What the OSD can do

  • FPS & frametime graph – measured precisely via Intel's PresentMon, not estimated.
  • GPU: load, temperature, clock, power draw, VRAM – NVIDIA, AMD and Intel, including multi-GPU systems (display GPU selectable).
  • CPU: load, temperature, clock, power draw, RAM – sensors run through the signed open-source driver PawnIO (the same foundation as LibreHardwareMonitor and FanControl), set up with a single click.
  • 6 designs, from subtle to detailed – plus a free choice of which values appear per category.
  • Live editing in-game: change position, design and transparency right on top of your running game – with mouse or gamepad, no alt-tabbing.
  • Hotkey or gamepad combo to toggle – freely bindable, works mid-game.

Why no extra tool is needed

Getting an FPS display plus hardware monitoring used to mean combining several programs with separate configuration. Lumora ships the whole chain – measurement, display, controls – built into the launcher you use to start your games anyway. Already using Afterburner & co.? Both coexist peacefully.
